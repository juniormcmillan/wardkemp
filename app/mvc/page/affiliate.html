Why Housing Disrepair Claims?

It is estimated that there are thousands of people living in poor conditions in Council and Housing Association homes across the UK.

Problems such as leaks, damaged windows and mould are leading to health problems whilst landlords do nothing.
We are dedicated to levelling the playing field in the battle between landlords and tenants for whom housing disrepair has become an issue.

What is our affiliate partner program?

Our affiliate partner programme is just one of the methods we are using to draw attention to the issue of Housing Disrepair and is also a way for us to contact tenants who may be owed compensation.

We only need a few details about a tenant's situation to be able to successfully pursue the landlord for the compensation owed and with your help, we can get this message out to even more people.

How the program works

If you decide to become one of our affiliate partners, we will ask you to promote our services online using any combination of websites, blogs, newsletters or emails you wish to use.

The programme is entirely free to join, so there is no financial risk. Simply place banner adverts (we have already created these for you) on your website or blog and then promote them to as wide an audience as possible.

The more people who see the adverts, the more potential claimants you're likely to reach. The banner ads direct the potential client to our website (www.wardkemp.uk) where they will be able to find out if they have a valid claim for compensation. For every successful claim we process, you will receive £100.

Register an account to access adverts made by us
Use social media to share the adverts promoting our service
For each claim accepted by our Solicitor panel, you receive £100
Below is a list of issues that people can potentially claim for, so if you know anyone who is a Council or Housing Association tenant and is suffering with any of these problems, share one of our adverts with them.

  • Blown or damaged plaster
  • Electrical problems
  • Leaks in roof
  • Damaged windows or doors including failed double glazing
  • Infestation
  • Issues with boiler or heating system
  • Major cracks in wall or render
  • Major damp or mould
  • Damaged floorboards
