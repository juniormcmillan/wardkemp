What is Housing Disrepair?

It is estimated that there are thousands of people living in poor conditions in Council and Housing Association homes across England and Wales.

Problems such as leaks, damaged windows and mould are leading to health problems whilst landlords do nothing.
We are dedicated to levelling the playing field in the battle between landlords and tenants for whom housing disrepair has become an issue.

Help us to locate people who need our help and earn money at the same time!

This programme is just one of the methods we are using to draw attention to the issue of Housing Disrepair and is also a way for us to contact tenants who may be owed compensation.

We only need a few details about a tenant's situation to be able to successfully pursue the landlord for the compensation owed and with your help, we can get this message out to even more people.

How the program works

If you decide to become one of our Partners, we will ask you to promote our services by simply posting our adverts (these are available inside your account once you have registered) using your own social media accounts.

Please note however, that we can only accept claims from properties in England and Wales.

The more people who see the adverts, the more potential claimants you're likely to reach. The website link that we provide you with will direct potential clients to our website (https://www.wardkemp.uk/hd2) where they will be able to find out if they have a valid claim for compensation. For every successful claim we process, you will receive £100.

The programme is entirely free to join, so there is no financial risk.

Below is a list of issues that people can potentially claim for, so if you know anyone who is a Council or Housing Association tenant and is suffering with any of these problems, share one of our adverts with them.

Commission payments

For each successful claim that you send to us, you will receive £100 once the case has been settled. Commissions are paid on the 15th of the month for any successful claim settled during the previous month. Please note that commissions can only be paid by bank transfer into a UK bank account.
